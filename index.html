Hi! I'm a social researcher and writer based in Brooklyn, NY.

As a researcher, I'm a sociologist specializing in qualitative methods, labor, and technology. My master's dissertation was an interview study that looked at how job-seekers use and encounter AI in their search. I write about issues of AI and labor as a freelance contributor for O'Reilly Media's Radar publication. I have a B.A. in Sociology from Barnard College of Columbia University, and an M.Sc. in Sociology from The London School of Economics.

As a writer, in addition to work drawing on my research, I am a produced and published playwright (and painfully unproduced screenwriter). I have had productions all over the United States and been awarded the Lin-Manuel Miranda Family Fellowship, The Dasha Amsterdam Epstein Award in Playwriting, and has been a semi-finalist for The Bay Area Playwrights Festival. I studied playwriting at The National Theater Institute. You can read my plays on New Play Exchange.

I am so committed to studying work and employment that I have been doing an autoethnography be being unemployed. Or so I tell myself I am actively looking for employment, specifically in areas of research, writing, and editorial.

For fun, you can catch me performing musical improv every Thursday with Backseat Driver at Magnet Theater.

Research

I am most interested in looking at work, technology, culture, and the intersection of all three. I draw primarily from sociology but also from fields such as STS, psychology, and economics. My most recent research project looked at how upcoming grads are using and encountering generative AI in their job search. While especially interested in qualitative methods, I am also trained in quantitative methods, specifically using R for social data science.

Playwriting etc.

Below are selected plays and other creative projects. For full drafts, you can either read with a New Play Exchange, or just email me at anjalir@gmail.com and I'll very happily send them to you. For more details on experiences and productions, you can go to my resum here.

Sense and Sensibility: 1976

Sisters Elinor and Marianne Dashwood have just lost their father, resulting not only in grief but financial ruin. Forced to move out of their home and adjust to a new normal, the Dashwoods navigate social change, expectations, and classic Jane Austen romances in this adaptation that reimagines them as women of color living in New York City in the summer of 1976.

Produced by Columbia University Players in November 2022.

Available for purchasing and licensing at Next Stage Press.

Scars of Metamorphosis

The show's Protagonist's attempts to overcome trauma from a near fatal heart attack at 16 by dissecting storytelling tropes, but falters in the real world just when she thinks she's conquered her past, all despite her self-perceived awareness of fantasies. A meta-theatrical play, we follow the Protagonist as she introduces us to her past and present, in which she is the finalist for a writing competition where she meets threats her happy ending.

Produced by Columbia University NOMADS in November 2021.
Semi-Finalist, Bay Area Playwrights Festival, 2025

Shelf life, a bookstore play

In an independent bookstore in suburban Boston, employees deal with a bookfluencer hire, a boss that refuses to rest his back, a customer that camps out on the info desk, and a job that does not love them as much as they love the idea of it.

career play

Does your resum have keywords to get through AI scanners!? Did you make sure to hint you want a job without asking for it because that would be overbearing!? Are you aware that everything that happens to you is completely your fault and no one else's!? career play centers three unemployed, burned out aspiring career warriors from different walks of life being bombarded with these questions in a career coaching program, where they assemble in hopes of finally cracking the secret to getting hired in the humiliation simulation of the current job market.

Pledge of The Regent's (Pilot)

In this mockumentary style dramedy, a documentary filmmaker has finally been given his big break...to singlehandedly film a documentary about Resurgence, an elite Regency matchmaking retreat in the English countryside that boasts a high marriage rate, celebrity alumni, and skeptical 20 something from English speaking countries hoping to meet their match.
